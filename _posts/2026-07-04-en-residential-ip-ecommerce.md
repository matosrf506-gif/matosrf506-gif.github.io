## Why IP Type Matters for Marketplace and Ad Accounts

Online platforms build trust profiles for every account on their systems. These trust profiles determine whether your account operates with full capabilities, gets flagged for review, or gets restricted entirely.

IP address signals feed directly into this trust calculation:

- **Where is the account being accessed from?** A US-registered seller account accessed from a datacenter IP in Singapore raises immediate flags.
- **Is this IP associated with other accounts?** Shared IP pools mean your IP may carry the history of hundreds of other users, including those who violated platform policies.
- **Is the IP behavior consistent with a real business?** Real companies access their accounts from stable, consistent IP addresses — not rotating proxy pools.

The type of IP you use determines how you answer all three of these questions.

## Residential IP vs. Datacenter IP: The Core Difference

| Dimension | Datacenter IP | Shared Residential IP | Native Residential IP (Dedicated) |
|-----------|--------------|----------------------|-----------------------------------|
| IP Origin | Cloud/VPS providers (AWS, Alibaba Cloud, etc.) | ISP residential networks, shared pool | ISP residential networks, dedicated to one user |
| Platform Identification | Immediately flagged as non-human / proxy | Treated as residential, but carries shared history | Indistinguishable from a genuine local user |
| Account Association Risk | Extreme — datacenter IPs link accounts instantly | High — shared IPs connect your account to others' violations | Minimal — your IP, your history only |
| Behavioral Consistency | Inconsistent with real business behavior | Inconsistent due to IP rotation | Consistent with genuine business operations |
| Suitable for Cross-Border Business | No | Limited | Yes — recommended |

The distinction between shared residential and native (dedicated) residential IP deserves special attention. A shared residential IP comes from real ISP infrastructure, which is better than a datacenter IP — but the IP is cycled across many users. If another user in that pool ran a policy-violating ad campaign last week, that history is attached to the IP when it rotates to you. You inherit someone else's risk record.

A **dedicated native residential IP** means the IP is assigned exclusively to your account. Its history is only what you've done with it. For marketplace sellers and ad buyers who need clean, predictable IP environments, this is the meaningful difference.

## What Residential IP Does Not Replace

IP quality is a foundational layer of account protection — not a complete solution on its own. Alongside a dedicated residential IP, cross-border accounts also require:

- **Separate payment methods** per account: shared billing information is a strong association signal independent of IP
- **Clean device environments**: anti-detect browsers or separate physical devices prevent browser fingerprint associations
- **Policy-compliant content**: IP quality improves account trust scores but does not override content violations
- **Consistent account behavior**: unusual spikes in activity (such as mass following or rapid campaign scaling) can trigger reviews regardless of IP quality

The combination of a clean residential IP with disciplined account hygiene practices provides the most robust protection for cross-border accounts.
